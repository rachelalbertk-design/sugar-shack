# Sugar Shack Story Roadmap

Launch target: seven complete shifts.

The story should feel like one escalating week below the donut shop, not a set of disconnected cases. Every shift should preserve the existing engine:

scene -> choice -> stat/flag update -> consequence -> next scene -> debrief

## Design Spine

The repeated question:

Do you serve what someone ordered, what they meant, or what they needed?

Core player verbs:

- Serve
- Alter
- Delay
- Refuse

Stats stay hidden during gameplay. The player should read faces, coded drinks, civilian behavior, receipt notes, and room pressure instead of optimizing visible numbers.

## Seven-Shift Arc

### Shift 1 - Whiskey Ramen

Status: playable and polished for testing.

Function: teach the wrong-glass problem and the human cost of coded orders.

Key civilians: Nia and Jules.

Core question: do you protect someone who copied danger without understanding it?

### Shift 2 - The Glass Orchid

Status: playable and ready for testing.

Function: introduce the next layer of coded drinks and the first visible Harmony slogan.

Pressure: a civilian interruption should make the player choose whether to protect cover, a patron, or the room's fragile calm.

### Shift 3 - The Memory Tab

Status: playable and ready for testing.

Function: make memory audits feel personal before they feel political.

Pressure: someone remembers the wrong version of a night at Sugar Shack, and someone else needs that mistake to stay alive.

### Shift 4 - Two Birthdays

Status: playable and ready for testing.

Function: turn public exposure into danger.

Pressure: a celebration, a photo, or a social post makes privacy feel like a thing civilians can break by accident.

### Shift 5 - The Kindness Audit

Status: playable and ready for testing.

Function: reveal Director Vale through a recorded message.

Pressure: kindness becomes measurable, weaponized, and suspiciously reasonable.

### Shift 6 - The House Leak

Status: playable and ready for testing.

Function: let Director Vale speak through the system and test the player's loyalty to the house.

Pressure: Sugar Shack itself may be compromised, and no one can tell whether secrecy is protection or control.

### Shift 7 - Last Call for Privacy

Status: playable and ready for testing.

Function: final bargain, final receipt, final psychology debrief.

Pressure: Director Vale offers safety through surveillance. The player decides what privacy is worth when people are genuinely scared.

## Launch Acceptance

The story is prototype-complete when:

- all seven shifts are playable end to end;
- earlier flags affect later scenes in visible, story-first ways;
- Director Vale escalates slowly and persuasively;
- civilians matter beyond one-off complications;
- every shift has at least four meaningful final outcomes;
- the final debrief reflects the player's whole run, not only Shift 7;
- the game can be replayed with noticeably different routes and receipts.

Current status: the seven-shift prototype is playable. The remaining launch work is editing, balance, accessibility testing, device testing, and native packaging.
